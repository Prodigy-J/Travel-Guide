Places

These are our top destinations.

London

England

A popular choice among our tourists. Definitely need to check it out if you haven't already.

London
London, the capital of England and the United Kingdom, is a 21st-century city with history stretching back to Roman times. At its centre stand the imposing Houses of Parliament, the iconic ‘Big Ben’ clock tower and Westminster Abbey, site of British monarch coronations. Across the Thames River, the London Eye observation wheel provides panoramic views of the South Bank cultural complex, and the entire city.

Currency: Pound sterling

Population: 8.982 million (2019)Official Language: English

Paris

France

France never ceases to avail to us amazing places to explore. Gotta check it out.

Paris
Also known as the Latin Quarter, the 5th arrondissement is home to the Sorbonne university and student-filled cafes. It's also known for its bookshops, including the famed Shakespeare & Company. Family-friendly attractions include the Jardin des Plantes botanical gardens and the National Museum of Natural History. The stately Panthéon building holds the remains of notables like Voltaire and Marie Curie.

Currency: Euro, CFP franc

Population: 2.161 million (2019)Official Language: French

Bangkok

Thailand

Looking for temples? This is definitely the place to find a lot.

China
Bangkok, Thailand’s capital, is a large city known for ornate shrines and vibrant street life. The boat-filled Chao Phraya River feeds its network of canals, flowing past the Rattanakosin royal district, home to opulent Grand Palace and its sacred Wat Phra Kaew Temple. Nearby is Wat Pho Temple with an enormous reclining Buddha and, on the opposite shore, Wat Arun Temple with its steep steps and Khmer-style spire.

Currency: Thai baht

Metro Population: 14,626,225Official Language: Thai

Hong Kong

China

Try going on a shopping spree in Hong Kong. Come back and thank me later.

Hong Kong
Hong Kong, officially the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China, is a city and special administrative region of China on the eastern Pearl River Delta in South China.

Currency: Renminbi

Population: 7.482 million (2020)Official Language: Mandarin

New York

USA

Visit the most populous city in the United States.

New York
New York City comprises 5 boroughs sitting where the Hudson River meets the Atlantic Ocean. At its core is Manhattan, a densely populated borough that’s among the world’s major commercial, financial and cultural centers. Its iconic sites include skyscrapers such as the Empire State Building and sprawling Central Park. Broadway theater is staged in neon-lit Times Square.

Currency: United States Dollars (USD)

Population: 8.419 million (2019)Official Language: English

Singapore City

Singapore

Singapore attracts travelers all year round. You could be one.

Singapore City
Singapore, officially the Republic of Singapore, is a sovereign island city-state in maritime Southeast Asia. Well-known landmarks include the Merlion, Marina Bay Sands, Gardens by the Bay, the Jewel, the Orchard Road shopping belt, the resort island of Sentosa, and the Singapore Botanic Gardens, Singapore's first UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Currency: Singapore dollar

Population: 5.686 million (2020)Official Languages: English, Malay, Tamil, Mandarin Chinese

Istanbul

Turkey

One of the world's famous cities, so it makes sense that people want to visit.

Istanbul
Istanbul is a major city in Turkey that straddles Europe and Asia across the Bosphorus Strait. Its Old City reflects cultural influences of the many empires that once ruled here. In the Sultanahmet district, the open-air, Roman-era Hippodrome was for centuries the site of chariot races, and Egyptian obelisks also remain. The iconic Byzantine Hagia Sophia features a soaring 6th-century dome and rare Christian mosaics.

Currency: Turkish lira

Population: 15.46 million (Dec 31, 2020)Official Language: Turkish

Tokyo

Japan

An exciting city that attracts millions every year.

Tokyo
Tokyo, Japan’s busy capital, mixes the ultramodern and the traditional, from neon-lit skyscrapers to historic temples. The opulent Meiji Shinto Shrine is known for its towering gate and surrounding woods. The Imperial Palace sits amid large public gardens. The city's many museums offer exhibits ranging from classical art (in the Tokyo National Museum) to a reconstructed kabuki theater (in the Edo-Tokyo Museum).

Currency: Japanese yen

Population: 13.96 million (2021)Official Language: Japanese

Cape Town

South Africa

One of the most beloved destinations on the continent.

Cape Town
Cape Town is a port city on South Africa’s southwest coast, on a peninsula beneath the imposing Table Mountain. Slowly rotating cable cars climb to the mountain’s flat top, from which there are sweeping views of the city, the busy harbor and boats heading for Robben Island, the notorious prison that once held Nelson Mandela, which is now a living museum.

Currency: South African rand

Population: 4.618 million (2020)Official Languages: Afrikaans, English, Xhosa, Zulu, Southern Sotho, Venda, Northern Sotho, Tswana, Tsonga, Swati, Ndebele

Giza

Egypt

This city offers a view you will never forget.

Giza
Giza is an Egyptian city on the west bank of the Nile, near Cairo. The Giza Plateau is home to iconic Egyptian monuments, including 3 tall pyramids built as royal mausoleums around the 26th century B.C. The largest, the Great Pyramid, is King Khufu’s tomb. The Great Sphinx is a vast sculpture of a man’s head on a lion’s body. The Solar Boat Museum displays a restored cedar barge found buried near the Great Pyramid.

Currency: Egyptian Pound

Population: 8.8 million (Oct 2018)Official Language: Modern Standard Arabic
